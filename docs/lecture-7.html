Variation of Parameters (Lagrange)

Limitation of Undetermined Coefficients:

  • Requires constant coefficients $P,Q$.
  • $R(x)$ must be exponential/polynomial/sin-cos (or products).
  • Fails for variable coefficients, e.g., $y'' + y = \tan x$.

New method works for the general nonhomogeneous equation

$$ y'' + P(x)y' + Q(x)y = R(x) \tag{1} $$

where $P,Q,R$ are continuous on open interval $I$.

Assumption: Know two linearly independent solutions $y_1,y_2$ of the homogeneous equation

$$ y'' + P(x)y' + Q(x)y = 0 \tag{2} $$

Goal: Find particular solution of form

$$ y_p(x) = v_1(x) y_1(x) + v_2(x) y_2(x) \tag{3} $$

(vary the constants $c_1,c_2$ into functions $v_1,v_2$).

Derivation

Differentiate (3):

$$ y_p' = v_1' y_1 + v_1 y_1' + v_2' y_2 + v_2 y_2' $$

Impose condition (avoids second derivatives of $v_i$):

$$ v_1' y_1 + v_2' y_2 = 0 \tag{4} $$

Then

$$ y_p'' = v_1' y_1' + v_1 y_1'' + v_2' y_2' + v_2 y_2'' $$

Substitute into (1). Homogeneous parts cancel, leaving

$$ v_1' y_1' + v_2' y_2' = R(x) \tag{5} $$

Solve system (4)+(5) using Wronskian $W = y_1 y_2' - y_1' y_2 \neq 0$:

$$ v_1' = -\frac{y_2 R}{W}, \quad v_2' = \frac{y_1 R}{W} $$

Integrate:

$$ v_1 = -\int \frac{y_2 R}{W}\, dx, \quad v_2 = \int \frac{y_1 R}{W}\, dx $$

Particular solution:

$$ y_p = y_2 \int \frac{y_1 R}{W}\, dx - y_1 \int \frac{y_2 R}{W}\, dx $$

General solution of (1):

$$ y = c_1 y_1 + c_2 y_2 + y_p $$

Example

$$ y'' + y = \sec x $$

Homogeneous: $y_1 = \cos x$, $y_2 = \sin x$, $W=1$

$$ v_1' = -\sin x \sec x = -\tan x \implies v_1 = \ln|\cos x| $$ $$ v_2' = 1 \implies v_2 = x $$ $$ y_p = \cos x \ln|\cos x| + x \sin x $$

General solution:

$$ y = c_1 \cos x + c_2 \sin x + \cos x \ln|\cos x| + x \sin x $$

Higher-Order Linear ODEs

General form:

$$ P_n(x) y^{(n)} + P_{n-1}(x) y^{(n-1)} + \cdots + P_0(x) y = R(x) $$

Standard form:

$$ y^{(n)} + p_{n-1}(x) y^{(n-1)} + \cdots + p_0(x) y = R(x) $$
  • Homogeneous if $R\equiv 0$.
  • A solution is an $n$-times differentiable function satisfying the equation identically.

Linearity Principle: Linear combinations of homogeneous solutions are solutions.

Linear Independence (n functions on $I$):
$c_1 y_1 + \cdots + c_n y_n \equiv 0 \implies$ all $c_i=0$.

Basis (Fundamental set): $n$ linearly independent solutions.

General solution (homogeneous): $y_h = c_1 y_1 + \cdots + c_n y_n$.
General solution (nonhomogeneous): $y = y_p + y_h$.

Initial Value Problem (n-th Order)

$$ y(x_0)=y_0,\ y'(x_0)=y_1,\ \dots,\ y^{(n-1)}(x_0)=y_{n-1} $$

Existence & Uniqueness:
If coefficients $p_{n-1},\dots,p_0$ continuous on open $I\ni x_0$, then unique solution on entire $I$.

Wronskian for n Functions

$$ W(y_1,\dots,y_n)(x) = \det\begin{vmatrix} y_1 & \cdots & y_n \\ y_1' & \cdots & y_n' \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ y_1^{(n-1)} & \cdots & y_n^{(n-1)} \end{vmatrix} $$

Theorem (solutions of linear homogeneous ODE with continuous coefficients):
The $n$ solutions are linearly dependent on $I$ ‚áî $W\equiv 0$ on $I$.
If $W(x_0)\neq 0$, they form a basis on $I$.

Homogeneous Constant-Coefficient Equations

$$ y^{(n)} + a_{n-1} y^{(n-1)} + \cdots + a_0 y = 0 $$

Characteristic equation:

$$ r^n + a_{n-1} r^{n-1} + \cdots + a_0 = 0 $$

Basis:

  • Distinct real roots $r_k$: $\{e^{r_k x}\}$
  • Real root $r$ multiplicity $m$: $\{e^{rx}, x e^{rx}, \dots, x^{m-1} e^{rx}\}$
  • Simple complex $\alpha\pm i\beta$: $\{e^{\alpha x}\cos\beta x, e^{\alpha x}\sin\beta x\}$
  • Complex multiplicity $m$: Multiply by $x^k$ ($k=0,\dots,m-1$) for cos/sin terms.

Nonhomogeneous ‚Äì Undetermined Coefficients (Higher Order)

Same Basic Rule, Sum Rule, Modification Rule as 2nd order.

Modification: Multiply guessed term by smallest power of $x$ so it is independent of homogeneous solution.

Example:

$$ y''' + 3y'' + 3y' + y = 30 e^{-x} $$

with $y(0)=3$, $y'(0)=-3$, $y''(0)=-47$.
Characteristic: $(r+1)^3=0$ (triple root $-1$).
$y_h = (c_1 + c_2 x + c_3 x^2)e^{-x}$
Guess $y_p = C x^3 e^{-x}$ (√ó$x^3$).
Substitute into ODE ‚Üí solve for $C$.

Summary of Lecture 7

  • Variation of Parameters: General method using two homogeneous solutions and Wronskian. Formula: $y_p = y_2\int\frac{y_1 R}{W}\,dx - y_1\int\frac{y_2 R}{W}\,dx$.
  • Higher-order theory: Wronskian detects independence; existence/uniqueness when coefficients continuous.
  • Constant coefficients: Characteristic polynomial ‚Üí basis according to root type (real/complex, multiplicity).
  • Nonhomogeneous: $y = y_h + y_p$; undetermined coefficients with modification for higher multiplicity.
