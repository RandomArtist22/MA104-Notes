1. Linear First-Order ODEs

Recall
Explicit form: $y' = f(x,y)$

Linear if $f(x,y)$ is linear in $y$:
$f(x,y) = a(x)y + b(x)$

Standard form:

$$ y' + P(x)y = Q(x) \tag{1} $$

Simplest Case (P, Q constant)

$$ y' + py = q \quad (p \neq 0) $$

Separate variables:

$$ \frac{dy}{y - q/p} = -p\, dx $$ $$ \ln |y - q/p| = -p x + \ln |C| $$ $$ y = C e^{-p x} + \frac{q}{p} $$

General Case ‚Äì Method of Integrating Factor (Leibniz)

Multiply (1) by $\mu(x)$ so left side becomes exact derivative:

$$ \frac{d}{dx} [\mu(x) y] = \mu(x) Q(x) $$

How to find $\mu(x)$?
Require:

$$ \mu' y + \mu (P y) = \mu' y + \mu P y \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{\mu'}{\mu} = P(x) $$ $$ \ln |\mu| = \int P(x)\, dx + C_1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \mu(x) = e^{\int P(x)\, dx} $$

(We take $C_1=0$ without loss of generality.)

General Solution:

$$ y(x) = e^{-\int P\, dx} \left[ \int Q(x) e^{\int P\, dx}\, dx + C \right] \tag{2} $$

Example 1

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{y}{x} = 3x \quad (x \neq 0) $$

$P(x) = 1/x$, $Q(x) = 3x$
$\int P\, dx = \ln |x|$ ‚Üí $\mu(x) = x$

Multiply:

$$ x y' + y = 3x^2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{d}{dx}(x y) = 3x^2 $$ $$ x y = x^3 + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{y = x^2 + \frac{C}{x}} $$

Example 2 (IVP)

$$ y' + (\tan x) y = \sin 2x, \quad y(0)=1 $$

$P = \tan x$, $Q = \sin 2x$
$\int P\, dx = -\ln|\cos x|$ ‚Üí $\mu(x) = \sec x$

Multiply:

$$ \sec x\, y' + \sec x \tan x\, y = \sec x \sin 2x $$ $$ \frac{d}{dx}(\sec x\, y) = 2 \sin x $$ $$ \sec x\, y = -2 \cos x + C $$ $$ y = -2 \cos^2 x + C \cos x $$

Apply $y(0)=1$:

$$ 1 = -2(1) + C(1) \quad \Rightarrow \quad C=3 $$ $$ \boxed{y(x) = 3\cos x - 2\cos^2 x} $$

2. Bernoulli Equation

Form:

$$ y' + \alpha(x) y = R(x) y^\beta \tag{3} \quad (\beta \neq 0,1) $$

Note: When $\beta=0$ or $1$ ‚Üí already linear.

Transformation: Let $u = y^{1-\beta}$
Then $u' = (1-\beta) y^{-\beta} y'$

Substitute into (3) ‚Üí linear ODE in $u$:

$$ u' + (1-\beta)\alpha(x) u = (1-\beta) R(x) $$

Example: Logistic Equation

$$ y' = A y - B y^2 \quad (A,B>0) $$

Here $\beta=2$, $\alpha(x)=-A$, $R(x)=-B$
Let $u = y^{-1} = 1/y$ ‚Üí $u' = -y^{-2} y'$

$$ u' = - \frac{1}{y^2} (A y - B y^2) = -A u + B $$ $$ u' + A u = B $$

Integrating factor $e^{A x}$:

$$ u = \frac{B}{A} + C e^{-A x} $$ $$ y = \frac{1}{\frac{B}{A} + C e^{-A x}} = \frac{A}{B + C A e^{-A x}} $$

3. Existence and Uniqueness Theorems for 1st-Order IVP

IVP:

$$ y' = f(x,y), \quad y(x_0)=y_0 \tag{4} $$

Existence Theorem

If $f(x,y)$ is continuous and bounded ($|f| \leq K$) in rectangle
$D: |x-x_0| < a$, $|y-y_0| < b$,
then (4) has at least one solution on $|x-x_0| < \alpha$ where $\alpha = \min\{a, b/K\}$.

Uniqueness Theorem (Picard-Lindel√∂f)

If additionally $\partial f/\partial y$ is continuous and bounded ($|\partial f/\partial y| \leq M$) in $D$,
then (4) has at most one solution (hence exactly one by existence).

Equivalent (weaker) condition: $f$ satisfies Lipschitz condition in $y$:

$$ |f(x,y_1)-f(x,y_2)| \leq L |y_1-y_2| $$

Special Case: Linear ODE

For $y' + P(x)y = Q(x)$, if $P,Q$ continuous on $|x-x_0|unique solution on that interval.

Illustrative Examples

Ex 1: $y' = x^2 + y^2$, $y(1)=3$
$f$ and $\partial f/\partial y = 2y$ continuous everywhere ‚Üí unique solution exists.

Ex 2: $y' = \frac{x+y}{x-y}$, $y(x_0)=y_0$ with $x_0 \neq y_0$
$\partial f/\partial y$ blows up on $x=y$ ‚Üí but unique in rectangle not crossing $x=y$.

Ex 3: $y' = \frac13 x y^{2/5}$, $y(0)=-1$
Solution $y = (x^2 -1)^{5/3}$ valid on $\mathbb{R}$.
But on intervals crossing $|x|=1$, piecewise definitions show non-uniqueness outside $(-1,1)$.

Counterexample to uniqueness (only continuity):

$$ y' = \sqrt{|y|}, \quad y(0)=0 $$

Two solutions: $y\equiv 0$ and $y = \begin{cases} x^2/4 & x\geq0 \\ -x^2/4 & x<0 \end{cases}$

4. Orthogonal Trajectories

Definition: A family of curves that intersect a given family at right angles everywhere.

Angle of intersection = angle between tangents at intersection point.

Method (for one-parameter family $F(x,y,c)=0$):

  1. Differentiate w.r.t. $x$: $G(x,y,y',c)=0$
  2. Eliminate $c$ ‚Üí ODE $y' = f(x,y)$ for given family.
  3. Orthogonal family satisfies $y' = -1/f(x,y)$
  4. Solve the new ODE.

Example 1: Family of circles $x^2 + y^2 = c$

Differentiate: $2x + 2y y' = 0 \implies y' = -x/y$
Orthogonal: $y' = y/x$ ‚Üí $y = k x$ (lines through origin).

Visualization: Circles centered at origin ‚ä• radial lines.

Example 2: Family of ellipses $\frac12 x^2 + y^2 = c$ ($c>0$)

Differentiate: $x + 2 y y' = 0 \implies y' = -x/(2y)$
Orthogonal: $y' = 2y/x$
Separate: $\frac{dy}{y} = 2 \frac{dx}{x}$ ‚Üí $\ln|y| = 2\ln|x| + \ln k$
$\boxed{y^2 = k x^2}$? Wait, correction in notes: actually $y^2 = c x$ (parabolas).

Summary of Lecture 3

  • Linear 1st-order: Always solvable via integrating factor $\mu = e^{\int P\,dx}$.
  • Bernoulli: Reduce to linear via $u=y^{1-\beta}$.
  • Existence/Uniqueness: Sufficient conditions via continuity + boundedness of $f$ and $f_y$ (or Lipschitz).
  • Linear case: Guaranteed unique solution when coefficients continuous.
  • Orthogonal Trajectories: Practical application of ODEs ‚Äì replace slope by negative reciprocal.

Key Takeaway: These tools solve most first-order ODEs encountered in applications (growth, circuits, mixing, trajectories).
